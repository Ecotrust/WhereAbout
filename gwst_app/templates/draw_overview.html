Map Tool Overview

You are about to beging the 'map tool' portion of the survey. Before you begin, please take a moment to read over the following information. It will help prepare you to use a tool that may seem a little complex without it.

  • You will focus on only one {{interview.resource_name}} at a time, and only for one of the means by which you {{interview.resource_action}} it (i.e. only the salmon you fish via kayak).
  • You will be presented with a map on which to find your {{interview.shape_name_plural}}.
    • This survey is only interested in your {{interview.shape_name_plural}} along the Oregon Coast.
    • You will be able 'pan' across and 'zoom' in on the map to get a clearer view of your {{interview.shape_name_plural}}.

      • Maybe two pictures, one zoomed out, one in?

  • You will then use the tool to draw your {{interview.shape_name_plural}} on the map.
    • These grounds are the ones you deem important over your cumulative years of experience.

      • Some fishing grounds close together - illustrates accuracy, no penny values

  • You will then assign relative value to each {{interview.shape_name}} in the form of 'pennies'.
    • You will distribute 100 pennies across your {{interview.shape_name_plural}} for each {{interview.resource_name}}.
    • The more pennies you allocate to a {{interview.shape_name}}, the more important it is to you.
    • In the image below, the {{interview.shape_name}} with 50 pennies is more important to that person than either of the other two.

      • This picture is pretty much what I have in mind anyway.

    • Note that the pennies of the shapes all add up to 100.

Additional help can be found in our frequently asked questions page. Please do not hesitate to email fish@ecotrust.org if you need more help.
