Map Tool Overview

Please take a moment to read over the following information before you begin the mapping portion of this survey.


  • This survey is to map your {{interview.shape_name_plural}} along the Oregon Coast.
  • You will create a map for only one {{interview.resource_name}} at a time for each of your user groups (i.e. a map for salmon via kayak and a separate map for salmon via private vessel).

  • To begin you will be presented with a map of the Oregon Coast.
  • With this map, you will be able to 'move around' and 'zoom' into the areas where you'd like to draw your {{interview.shape_name_plural}}.

    • Maybe two pictures, one zoomed out, one in?


  • Once you zoom into your area you will then use the tool to draw your {{interview.shape_name_plural}} on the map.
    • These {{interview.shape_name_plural}} are the ones you deem important over your cumulative years of experience.

      • Some fishing grounds close together - illustrates accuracy, no penny values


  • You will then assign value to each {{interview.shape_name}} in the form of 'pennies'.
    • You will distribute 100 pennies across your {{interview.shape_name_plural}} by assigning pennies to each area you draw.
    • The more pennies you allocate to a {{interview.shape_name}}, the more important it is to you.
    • In the image below, the {{interview.shape_name}} with 50 pennies is more important to that person than either of the other two.

      • This picture is pretty much what I have in mind anyway.

    • Note that the pennies of the shapes all add up to 100.

  • You will follow this procedure to create a map for each {{interview.resource_name}} you fish for each user group (i.e. kayak, dive, or private vessel)

If you require any additional help or clarifications, please refer to our frequently asked questions page. Also, please do not hesitate to email fish@ecotrust.org if you need more help.
